Studio Dispatch

05 Mar 2026

AXWELLS shortlisted for European Public Form Award.

Our Copenhagen Transit Courtyard proposal entered the final three in infrastructure design. The jury highlighted its urban porosity strategy, where structural cores become daylight chimneys and public terraces operate as year-round social rooms.

The project was further praised for its passive climate shell, public sequencing, and low-maintenance structural rhythm across the courtyard edge.

↗
17 Feb 2026

Berlin studio launches low-carbon facade mock-up lab.

A full-scale test line now measures thermal response of mineral-based cladding systems.

The lab also benchmarks weathering, reflectance, and structural fixing tolerances before mock-ups are cleared for site deployment.

↗
28 Jan 2026

Tokyo office opens weekly public model critique evenings.

Emerging architects and students are invited to review process boards and physical studies.

Selected review sessions now feed directly into the office's research archive, helping test urban micro-density concepts against real critique.

AE

Aesop

Retail Atmosphere Systems

We co-developed sensory retail layouts where circulation, lighting gradients, and material touchpoints are orchestrated as one continuous architecture script. The result pairs ritualistic entry sequences, tactile product thresholds, and a more cinematic relation between display and shadow.

VI

Vitra

Workplace Spatial Research

Prototype workplace floors were tested with adaptable partitions and acoustic terraces to improve team focus without reducing social permeability. Our studies combined furniture micro-zones with architectural shell logic so teams could shift between concentration, review, and collective assembly without friction.

B&O

Bang & Olufsen

Audio-Responsive Interiors

Joint interior studies translated acoustic targets into geometry, integrating hidden resonance cavities and tactile sound diffusion surfaces. The collaboration shaped hospitality lounges and listening rooms where wall depth, ceiling rhythm, and object placement all contribute to the sound profile.

CO

Cosentino

Material Innovation

We tested engineered stone assemblies for high-traffic public zones, optimizing edge detailing and lifecycle durability while keeping a minimal visual expression. That work informed bench systems, stair transitions, and integrated reception volumes with stronger maintenance resilience.
